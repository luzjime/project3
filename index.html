Luz's Favorite City: Athens, Greece.

Why is Athens My Favorite City?

Athens, the capital of Greece, is one of the oldest cities in the world. With over 3,000 years of history, it's filled with ancient ruins, classical art, and mythological significance. I love the ancient myths that are tied to Greece, even moreso Athens, Greece. I love Athens for its deep cultural roots and its beautiful blend of the old and the modern. It's impact is known worldwide as well!

The Acropolis

The Acropolis is an ancient citadel perched above the city, and it is home to several iconic buildings of classical Greece--most famously the Parthenon. It’s a symbol of democracy and civilization and has withstood the test of time repeatedly. This building has seen wars, historical events, and even tourists.

The Plaka District

The Plaka District is the oldest neighborhood in Athens. Its charming narrow streets are filled with shops, cafes, and neoclassical homes. It’s a great place to stroll around and enjoy Greek culture up close. Most people are super nice and will be very welcoming, too. Also, there's some beautiful trees and flowers all around, so the views are stunning!

Temple of Olympian Poseidon

This majestic temple dedicated to the sea god Poseidon stands atop Cap Sounion, offering epic views of the Aegean Sea. Ever since it was built, it's been known as a major landmark for sailors returning home. Not as much now, but it now is probaby one of the most scenic spots in all of Greece.

Image:Wikimedia

The Parthenon

The Parthenon, dedicated to the goddess Athena--the goddess of Wisdom and Battle Strategy--sits atop the Acropolis and stands as a masterpiece of ancient architecture. It’s one of the most recognized monuments in the world. It is rumored that there used to be a statue of the goddess in the temple, but that over time, it disappeared. Various individuals speculate that it could've been stolen, destroyed, or even hidden. Nobody knows what happened to the statue, but they say it was absolutely stunning!

Some Quick Facts About Athens:

  • Population: ~3.1 million in the metro area
  • The language: Greek
  • It's known for: Philosophy, democracy, ancient ruins--especially the Parthenon!
  • The climate: Mediterranean, with dry summers and mild winters. They say it's similar to the weather that we have here in Washington State--excluding the heavy rain sometimes, of course.
